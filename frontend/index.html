5 Tips For A Safer Daily Commute

From checking your driver details before boarding to using the in-app panic button, small habits make a big difference. Here's how to stay safe on every ride, whether you're commuting to work or heading home late at night.

1. Always check your driver's name, car, and number plate against what's shown in the app before getting in.

2. Share your trip status with a friend or family member, especially for late-night rides.

3. Sit in the back seat where possible and keep the panic button easily accessible.

4. Keep your phone charged so live tracking and emergency contact features stay available throughout the trip.

5. Report anything unusual right after the ride so our team can look into it quickly.

How Ride Sharing Saves You Money

Splitting your commute with fellow riders can cut your monthly travel costs significantly. We break down how ride-sharing works, who it's best for, and how much you could realistically save.

When you share a ride with someone travelling a similar route, the fare is split between passengers instead of paid entirely by one rider - often cutting your cost noticeably compared to a solo trip.

It's a great fit for daily commuters with a predictable route and timing, students travelling to and from campus, and anyone looking to lower their monthly transport spend without giving up comfort or safety.

Combine it with our monthly pick-and-drop plan for an even more predictable, budget-friendly commute.
